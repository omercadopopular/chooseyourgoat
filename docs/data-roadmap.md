# Data roadmap

## Release gate

The yellow prototype warning may be removed only after all six players have match-level official appearances and goals, a reviewed competition taxonomy, tournament-edition records, source provenance and successful reconciliation tests.

## Source hierarchy

1. Governing-body, competition and club archives.
2. Open data with redistribution-compatible licenses, including OpenFootball match results and covered StatsBomb Open Data competitions.
3. Licensed commercial feeds with explicit derived-display rights.
4. RSSSF and specialist historical research for cross-checking, subject to copyright and attribution requirements.
5. Contemporary match sheets, newspapers, yearbooks and specialist books for unresolved early-career records.

No connector may scrape a source whose terms prohibit automated collection or redistribution.

## Six-player audit order

1. Lionel Messi and Cristiano Ronaldo: establish the modern structured-data connector and update process.
2. Ronaldo Nazário and Ronaldinho: test club/country identity and competition transitions.
3. Diego Maradona: resolve national competitions, friendlies and historical assist coverage.
4. Pelé: dedicated official-versus-all-documented reconciliation and early Brazilian competition taxonomy.

## Target canonical tables

- players and aliases;
- teams and historical identifiers;
- competitions and editions;
- matches and match status;
- player-match appearances/events;
- player-edition participation/trophies;
- sources, licenses and discrepancies.

## Quality badges

- **Verified:** complete official appearance and goal reconciliation, reviewed sources.
- **Substantial:** small enumerated gaps that do not enter covered aggregates.
- **Partial:** material field or career-span gaps.
- **Fixture:** synthetic prototype only.
